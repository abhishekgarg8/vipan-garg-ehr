## Why this report matters

## Treatment context newly filled from Drive

- A later Max 21 Aug 2025 discharge summary states that after the February 2024 relapse the patient received **6 cycles of carfilzomib + daratumumab + zoledronic acid + pomalidomide until `2024-09-04`**.
- The same discharge summary describes the September 2024 episode as acute kidney injury after that treatment phase, with anuric status and creatinine reported around `6.54 mg/dL` before transfer/ongoing care.

- This means the September 2024 renal hospitalization should be interpreted not only as a generic AKI event, but as a renal crisis temporally and pathologically linked in the records to carfilzomib exposure.

## Plain-English interpretation

This hospitalization marks a major deterioration outside the earlier remission/relapse pattern. The dominant issues in the extracted pages are acute kidney injury on chronic kidney disease, severe anemia, thrombocytopenia, and pleural fluid accumulation. The newly found Drive records add important causality context: the renal crisis followed a carfilzomib-containing post-relapse regimen, and the later biopsy report explicitly supports a post-carfilzomib TMA explanation. This report is highly relevant to later renal risk in 2025 and 2026.
