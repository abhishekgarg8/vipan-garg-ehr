### Possible Causes of Renal Decline
1. **Post-carfilzomib thrombotic microangiopathy** — later kidney biopsy described TMA and noted post-carfilzomib-induced TMA
2. **Multiple Myeloma itself** — possible light-chain kidney stress, though the biopsy summary did not document light-chain restriction in casts
3. **Treatment nephrotoxicity / renal stress** — zoledronic acid exposure and other treatment-related risks
4. **Aging + hypertension** — pre-existing risk factors
5. **Relapse** — active disease burden returning with light chain load

### Clinical Implications
- CKD G3b (eGFR 30–44) requires **dose adjustment** for renally cleared drugs (Bortezomib, certain antibiotics, contrast agents).
- CAR-T therapy eligibility may be affected by renal function.
- Zoledronic acid use should be re-evaluated (generally not recommended when eGFR < 30).
- Beta-2 microglobulin (B2M) values of 7864–10244 ng/mL in 2025 reflect BOTH renal impairment AND high disease burden (B2M is cleared by kidneys).
