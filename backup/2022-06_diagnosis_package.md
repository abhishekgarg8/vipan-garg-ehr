## Why this report matters

This is the key diagnostic package establishing multiple myeloma, its subtype, and the initial disease burden.

## Core findings

- Multiple myeloma diagnosed in June 2022
- Bone marrow plasma cells reported around `15%`
- Bone marrow described as hypocellular overall, around `15-20%` cellularity
- Bone lesions seen in spine and ribs, with D1 and D6 compression/collapse described in summary history
- FISH showed extra copies of `1q21 (CKS1B)` and `1p32 (CDKN2C)` in about `10%` of cells
- No evidence of del17p/TP53 deletion or common high-risk IGH translocations listed in the report set

## Plain-English interpretation

This report documents a high initial IgG-kappa myeloma burden with clear monoclonal protein production and marked free light chain elevation. The skeletal involvement and vertebral compression fractures make this more than a laboratory-only diagnosis.
